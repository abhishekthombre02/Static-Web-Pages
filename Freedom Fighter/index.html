Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose

Strong and Dedicated Leader who fight for Hindustan(Bharat)

Here is timeline of netaji subhash chandra bose

  • 1897 - Subhas Chandra Bose is born in Cuttack, Odisha, India.
  • 1819 - Bose clears the Indian Civil Service (ICS) examination but resigns in 1921, influenced by the non-cooperation movement led by Mahatma Gandhi.
  • 1921 - Bose joins the Indian National Congress and becomes an active member of the independence movement.
  • 1838 - Bose is elected as the President of the Indian National Congress at the Haripura Session.
  • 1839 - Due to ideological differences with the Congress leadership, particularly Mahatma Gandhi and Jawaharlal Nehru, Bose resigns from the Congress presidency.
  • 1840 - Bose forms the Forward Bloc, a political group within the Congress, advocating more aggressive action against British colonialism.
  • 1841 - Bose escapes house arrest and flees to Germany via Afghanistan and the Soviet Union.
  • 1841-1943- Bose seeks international support for India's independence and establishes the Indian National Army (INA) with Japanese support.
  • 1843 - Bose gives his famous "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!" speech in Singapore.
  • 1845 - Bose's death remains a subject of debate and mystery. According to the official version, he died in a plane crash in Taihoku (now Taipei) on August 18, 1945. However, many conspiracy theories suggest otherwise, and some believe he survived the crash.
  • 1857 - The Government of India establishes the Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata in his honor.
